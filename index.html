Why DPL in 2026

Where Cricket Meets Data Science

India produces the world's largest volume of cricket data — yet has no dedicated competitive analytics platform. DPL is that platform: an open-access annual competition for data scientists, researchers, sports professionals, and cricket enthusiasts.

We're not looking for dashboards or retrospective stats. We want scalable, AI-ready products that coaches, franchise analysts, and broadcasters could use tomorrow — solutions that outthink, not just describe.

Top finalists present live at the Indian Sports Management Conference, Bangalore — December 2026, judged by analysts, franchise leaders, and academics.

The Inspiration

DPL is modelled on the NFL Big Data Bowl, which has placed 75+ analysts directly into professional sports roles. We apply the same rigorous, industry-connected philosophy to the IPL ecosystem — and to cricket's unique strategic depth.

Competition Format

What You Submit

DPL demands real, reproducible, explainable cricket science — not just ideas. Every submission has four mandatory components.

📋

Project Report

4–6 pages covering problem framing, methodology, key findings, limitations, and practical application — readable by technical and cricket audiences alike.

💻

Code Repository

Clean, reproducible code on GitHub with a README, data processing steps, model scripts, and full instructions to reproduce outputs.

📊

Presentation Deck

5–10 slides. Finalists present 5 minutes to an industry audience at the Indian Sports Management Conference, Bangalore.

🎮

Demo / Visual Output

Encouraged: an interactive dashboard, web app, coach-facing report card, or simulation tool that communicates your insight directly.

📡

Hosted on Kaggle

The competition runs on Kaggle (or equivalent), ensuring global reach, verified leaderboard integrity, and accessible data pipelines.

🌍

Open Globally

Individuals and teams may enter. Students in India may participate in both Student and Open categories. All submissions in English.

Evaluation

How Entries Are Judged

A composite score across six parameters, weighted by real-world cricket decision-making importance. The best entry won't be the most complex — it'll be the most right.

Cricket Insight & Problem Framing

10%

A clear, high-value cricket question with tactical or strategic relevance — and an explanation of why existing metrics fall short.

Use of Data

20%

Intelligent use of ball-by-ball, wagon-wheel, and/or line-and-length data. Stronger submissions combine multiple granular sources.

Analytical Rigour

20%

Robust, well-validated methodology. Handles uncertainty, sample size, confounding, and model limitations explicitly.

Originality & Innovation

20%

A fresh perspective, metric, or model on cricket analytics — something that wouldn't have been obvious from existing literature.

Practical Application

20%

Clearly actionable output. A coach, analyst, or auction strategist could use it directly. Specific, context-aware recommendations.

Communication & Presentation

10%

Polished, persuasive presentation with strong visuals. Technical methods explained in accessible cricket language.

Frequently Asked

Questions

Can I participate as an individual, or do I need a team?
Both work. You can enter as a solo participant or as a team. Team size limits and composition details will be released with the full challenge brief on 20 July 2026.
How many tracks can my team enter?
Each team may submit entries for up to three problem tracks. Entry fees apply per track, so choose the tracks that best match your skills and interests.
Can Indian students enter the Open Category?
Yes. Indian students may participate in both the Student Category and the Open Category, but they'll be evaluated against the standard of whichever category they enter. Separate entry fees apply for each.
What data will participants receive?
All participants receive access to a ball-by-ball IPL dataset including wagon-wheel (shot location) and line-and-length data. No external datasets are permitted in submissions.
Do I need to be in cricket or sports to participate?
Not at all. DPL is open to data scientists, ML engineers, statisticians, researchers, and analysts from any background. Cricket knowledge helps with problem framing, but analytical rigour is the core of evaluation.
What analytical methods are acceptable?
Any appropriate method — regression, classification, Bayesian models, clustering, simulation, causal inference, sequence modelling, explainable AI, or interactive visual analytics. The right method for the cricket question matters more than complexity.
What happens if my team makes it to the finals?
Top 3 finalists from each category are invited to present their work live at the Indian Sports Management Conference in Bangalore, December 2026. Travel and logistics details will be communicated to finalists in November.
When do registrations open and how do I register?
Registrations open on 20 July 2026. Until then, express your interest or ask questions by emailing outreach@insma.in — you'll be notified as soon as registration goes live.
Is there mentorship or support during the competition?
Yes. The active competition window (August–September) includes mentorship sessions and mid-point Q&A with the DPL team and industry advisors to help participants refine their approaches.
Are there opportunities beyond the prize money?
Yes. High-performing participants and finalists gain visibility with IPL franchises, sports analytics firms, and broadcasters who will be present at the Indian Sports Management Conference. Short-to-long-term partnerships with INSMA and its network are available to standout teams.
What is the submission format and deadline?
Submissions close on 15 October 2026 and must include a project report, GitHub code repository, presentation deck, and (where applicable) a demo or visual output. Full submission guidelines will be released with the challenge brief.
How are ties in scoring resolved?
In case of equal composite scores, the team with a higher score in the higher-weighted parameter(s) takes priority. The judging panel's decision is final.
