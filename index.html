Why DPL in 2026

Where Cricket Meets Data Science

India produces the world's largest volume of cricket data, yet has no dedicated competitive analytics platform. DPL is that platform: an open-access annual competition for data scientists, researchers, sports professionals, and cricket enthusiasts.

We're not looking for dashboards or retrospective stats. We want scalable, AI-ready products that coaches, franchise analysts, and broadcasters could use tomorrow — solutions that outthink, not just describe.

Top finalists present live at the Indian Sports Management Conference, Bangalore on 21-22 December 2026, judged by analysts, franchise leaders, and academics.

The Inspiration

DPL is modelled on the NFL Big Data Bowl, which has placed 75+ analysts directly into professional sports roles. We apply the same rigorous, industry-connected philosophy to the IPL ecosystem — and to cricket's unique strategic depth.

Competition Format

What You Submit

DPL demands real, reproducible, explainable cricket science — not just ideas. Every submission has four mandatory components.

📋

Project Report

4–6 pages covering problem framing, methodology, key findings, limitations, and practical application — readable by technical and cricket audiences alike.

💻

Code Repository

Clean, reproducible code on GitHub with a README, data processing steps, model scripts, and full instructions to reproduce outputs.

📊

Presentation Deck

5–10 slides. Finalists present 5 minutes to an industry audience at the Indian Sports Management Conference, Bangalore.

🎮

Demo / Visual Output

Encouraged: an interactive dashboard, web app, coach-facing report card, or simulation tool that communicates your insight directly.

📡

Hosted on Kaggle

The competition runs on Kaggle (or equivalent), ensuring global reach, verified leaderboard integrity, and accessible data pipelines.

🌍

Open Globally

Individuals and teams may enter. Students in India may participate in both Student and Open categories. All submissions in English.

Evaluation

How Entries Are Judged

A composite score across six parameters, weighted by real-world cricket decision-making importance. The best entry won't be the most complex, it'll be the most right.

Cricket Insight & Problem Framing

10%

A clear, high-value cricket question with tactical relevance — and an explanation of why existing metrics fall short.

Use of Data

20%

Intelligent use of ball-by-ball, wagon-wheel, and/or line-and-length data. Stronger submissions combine multiple granular sources.

Analytical Rigour

20%

Robust, well-validated methodology. Handles uncertainty, sample size, confounding, and model limitations explicitly.

Originality & Innovation

20%

A fresh perspective, metric, or model on cricket analytics — something that wouldn't be obvious from existing literature.

Practical Application

20%

Clearly actionable output. A coach, analyst, or auction strategist could use it directly. Specific, context-aware recommendations.

Communication & Presentation

10%

Polished, persuasive presentation with strong visuals. Technical methods explained in accessible cricket language.

Frequently Asked

Questions

Can I participate as an individual, or do I need a team?
Both work. You can enter solo or as a team. Team size limits will be released with the full challenge brief on 20 July 2026.
How many tracks can my team enter?
Each team may submit entries for up to three problem tracks. Entry fees apply per track, so choose the tracks that best match your strengths.
Can Indian students enter the Open Category?
Yes. Indian students may enter both categories, evaluated against the standard of whichever they enter. Separate entry fees apply for each.
What data will participants receive?
All participants receive a ball-by-ball IPL dataset including wagon-wheel (shot location) and line-and-length data. No external datasets are permitted.
Do I need a cricket background to participate?
Not at all. DPL is open to data scientists, ML engineers, statisticians, and analysts from any field. Analytical rigour is the core of evaluation.
What analytical methods are acceptable?
Any — regression, classification, Bayesian models, causal inference, sequence modelling, explainable AI, visual analytics. The right method for the question matters more than complexity.
What happens if my team reaches the finals?
Top 3 finalists from each category present live at the Indian Sports Management Conference, Bangalore, December 2026. Travel details communicated in November.
When do registrations open?
Registrations open 20 July 2026. Express interest now by emailing outreach@insma.in — you'll be notified as soon as registration goes live.
Is there mentorship during the competition?
Yes. The August–September window includes mentorship sessions and mid-point Q&A with the DPL team and industry advisors.
Are there opportunities beyond prize money?
Yes. Finalists gain visibility with IPL franchises, sports analytics firms, and broadcasters at the conference. Partnerships with INSMA's network are available to standout teams.
What is the submission deadline?
Submissions close 15 October 2026 and must include a project report, GitHub repository, presentation deck, and (where applicable) a demo output.
How are ties in scoring resolved?
In case of equal composite scores, the team with higher scores on higher-weighted parameters takes priority. The judging panel's decision is final.
